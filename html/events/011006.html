Notes:

Last year was complicated with two different divisions, using two different sets of timing, with different numbers of rounds, and two different tournament directors. It was like we were running two different tournaments. After another year of hosting tournaments, I think we can simplify pretty easily.

All players, kyu and dan, will have the same number of rounds, and the same clocks. The consensus before was that 60-minute rounds was the quickest possible for playing with the big-boys, and we will retain that. Both Salem and Corvallis tournaments used 60-minute clocks. Portland T, though, will use overtime. Last year we did 5 periods of 30-seconds for ING clocks; this year we will try 5 periods of 40-second clocks (I recall at least two matches last year that were called on account of time). I am arranging for more ING clocks; hopefully we will have enough, if not, we'll work it out.

Using 60 minute clocks, plus overtime, I guestimate the max time for each round to be 2 hours, 30 minutes.

I've made preliminary arrangements for generous fundmatching from the ING Foundation and the AGA. More clocks, stones, and boards have been requested. Also, the Corvallis Go Club is attempting to make ~25 game boards for tournament use. I've continued to be in contact with Mr. Robbins, author of the WinTD software, and have used new versions of it in Salem, and Corvallis, since Portland last year.

The last two tournaments since Portland have seen a shift away from concentrated cash prizes to a wider distribution of cash and merchandise. In Corvallis, literaly every player was able to recieve some form of prize. Cash will still be used in Portland, with emphasis on the dan players. For those of you who wish to play without prizes, please arrange to donate your prizes to the Corvallis Go Club :)))) Resources are always appreciated :)

Regarding general awards, I started thinking about delivery of a prize for the player with the most tournament wins in the Oregon tournament circuit (Salem - Corvallis - Portland). Right now I believe Mr. Tsukamoto leads, but I need to do the tally again. Other awards of acknowlegment are a good idea and will be considered.

This year I will try tending this tournament as the solo TD as part of the simplifications. My extreme thanks to Michael Alford on the prior two tournaments in Portland. Without his impetus two years ago, this tournament would not be in place today. And, without his volunteering his time away from work, the previous two Portland tournaments wouldn't have happened. Oregon players, and Portland players in particular, received a great benefit that day when Michael checked in with Albyn to see if we could have a tournament at Reed College.
